# Magnifica Humanitas

On **25 May 2026**, **Pope Leo XIV** released **Magnifica Humanitas** ("On Safeguarding the Human
Person in the Time of Artificial Intelligence"), his first encyclical and the first devoted to
artificial intelligence.[^wiki-magnifica][^vatican] Signed on 15 May — the 135th anniversary of Leo
XIII's *Rerum Novarum* — the roughly **42,000-word** text argues that "technology is never neutral"
and calls for AI to be kept under vigilant ethical and political oversight in service of human
dignity.[^vatican][^wiki-magnifica] In an unusual step the Pope presented it personally, and the
audience included **[[Chris Olah]]**, a co-founder of [[Anthropic]], who described the Church as
"informed critics" and the moment as the start of "a long collaboration between those of us who are
building this and those who can see what we, from inside, cannot."[^wiki-magnifica]
