Alibaba is a Chinese multinational conglomerate founded on June 28, 1999, in Hangzhou, Zhejiang, by Jack Ma and 17 co-founders[^wikipedia-alibaba-group]. It's best known outside China as an e-commerce giant — its platforms include Taobao, Tmall, and AliExpress — but its business also spans logistics (Cainiao), digital media, and, most relevant here, cloud computing[^wikipedia-alibaba-group].

That cloud arm, **Alibaba Cloud** (also known by its Chinese name, **Aliyun**), launched in September 2009 to mark the group's tenth anniversary and has grown into the largest cloud provider in China and the wider Asia-Pacific region[^wikipedia-alibaba-cloud]. Alongside renting out servers and storage, Alibaba Cloud develops its own large language models — a product line it lists among its notable AI offerings[^wikipedia-alibaba-cloud].

## Tongyi and Qwen

Alibaba Cloud's large-model work is organized under **Tongyi Lab** (Tongyi Laboratory). In April 2023 the company introduced a beta large language model under the name **Tongyi Qianwen** — "Tongyi" roughly meaning "unified intelligence," with "Qianwen" giving rise to the shorter English name **[[Qwen]]** — and opened it to public use that September, after clearing Chinese regulatory review[^technode-tongyi-2023]. Qwen models are released under a mix of licenses, from fully open Apache 2.0 to a more restrictive, source-available "Qwen License" and non-commercial research terms[^wikipedia-qwen]. By 2024 the family had grown to more than 100 open-weight model releases with a combined download count above 40 million, and by May 2026 the consumer Qwen app had reached 234 million users[^wikipedia-qwen].

## Investments across China's AI labs

Beyond building its own models, Alibaba has taken equity stakes in several of China's prominent large-model startups, a pattern that has led commentators to group a cluster of these companies together. In fiscal year 2024 Alibaba invested roughly US$0.8 billion for about a 36% stake in [[Moonshot AI]], and co-led — alongside HongShan — Moonshot's February 2024 Series B round of more than $1 billion at a $2.5 billion valuation[^techcrunch-moonshot-2024]. The same March, Alibaba led a funding round of more than $600 million in [[MiniMax]], valuing the company above $2.5 billion[^fortune-minimax-2024]. And in October 2023, Alibaba joined Tencent and Xiaomi in a roughly $700 million (about 5 billion yuan) round for Baichuan Intelligence[^scmp-baichuan-2024].

These investments sit alongside rivals such as [[DeepSeek]], which — unlike Moonshot AI, MiniMax, and Baichuan — has developed its models ([[DeepSeek (models)|DeepSeek]]) without a comparable stake from Alibaba, illustrating how Alibaba's cloud and capital have become a hub connecting much of China's [[Open weights|open-weight]] model ecosystem.
