# Andrej Karpathy

**Andrej Karpathy** is a deep-learning researcher and a founding member of [[OpenAI]],
but he is just as well known for something less formal: teaching a generation of
engineers how large language models actually work. He has moved between the field's
leading labs while building some of its most widely used educational material.

## Career

Karpathy completed his PhD at Stanford in 2015 under Fei-Fei Li, then became a
**founding member of [[OpenAI]]**, where he was a research scientist from 2015 to
2017.[^karpathy-wiki] He left to lead AI and Autopilot vision at Tesla (2017–2022),
**returned to OpenAI in early 2023**, and departed again in early 2024 to found
**Eureka Labs**, an AI-education venture.[^karpathy-wiki]

## As an educator

Karpathy's largest influence on the LLM era may be pedagogical. He taught Stanford's
influential CS231n course and produced the much-watched **"Neural Networks: Zero to
Hero"** lecture series, including a from-scratch "Let's build GPT."[^karpathy-wiki] His
**nanoGPT** project — "the simplest, fastest repository for training/finetuning
medium-sized GPTs," compact enough to reproduce GPT-2 in a few hundred lines of code —
became a common way to learn the internals of the [[Transformer]] and [[GPT-3|GPT]]
models.[^nanogpt]

In February 2025 he also coined the term **"[[Vibe coding]]"** for the practice of
building software mostly by prompting an AI rather than writing code by
hand.[^karpathy-wiki]
