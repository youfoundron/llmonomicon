# Arthur Mensch

**Arthur Mensch** (born 1992) is a French AI researcher, the co-founder and CEO of
[[Mistral AI]], and the most prominent individual voice for the permissive open-weight
approach to building language models.[^mensch-wiki][^mensch-time]

## Background

Mensch trained as a researcher: after École Polytechnique and Télécom Paris, he earned
a PhD (2015–2018) at Inria, working on machine-learning methods for large-scale
functional-MRI analysis.[^mensch-wiki] In late 2020 he joined [[Google DeepMind|DeepMind]]'s
Paris office, where he spent nearly three years working on large language models,
multimodal systems, and retrieval-augmented architectures before leaving in
2023.[^mensch-wiki]

## Mistral AI

In 2023 Mensch co-founded **[[Mistral AI]]** with Guillaume Lample and Timothée
Lacroix, and became its CEO; the company's history and models are covered in its own
entry.[^mensch-wiki][^mensch-time] He is notable as a public advocate for open models:
he "advocates for the transparency of open-source models, which he considers a guarantee
of safety."[^mensch-wiki] That argument — that openly inspectable weights are safer than
closed ones — makes him a recognizable counterweight to the closed-API incumbents, and
the human face of the case for [[Open weights]].
