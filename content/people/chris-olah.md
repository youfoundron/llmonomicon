# Chris Olah

**Chris Olah** is a co-founder of [[Anthropic]] and one of the pioneers of
**mechanistic interpretability** — the research program that tries to
reverse-engineer what a neural network has actually learned.[^olah-wiki] He has
pursued that single thread across the field's major labs.

## An interpretability career

Olah worked on understanding neural networks "first at Google [Brain], then at OpenAI,
and later at Anthropic," which he co-founded in 2021.[^olah-wiki] His most influential
work is the **"Circuits"** thread published in the journal *Distill*, whose opening
article, "Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits" (2020), set out to reverse-engineer
vision models at the level of individual **features** and the **circuits** that connect
them.[^olah-circuits] That program of work helped define the subfield now known as
[[Mechanistic interpretability]].[^olah-wiki]

## At Anthropic

At Anthropic, Olah leads the interpretability effort — mapping the internal
"features" of large [[Transformer]] models — and is a prominent voice for the view
that genuinely understanding a model's internals is essential to making it
safe.[^olah-wiki] The organization's broader mission is covered in [[Anthropic]].
