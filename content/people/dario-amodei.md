# Dario Amodei

**Dario Amodei** (born 1983) is an American AI researcher and the co-founder and
CEO of [[Anthropic]].[^wiki-amodei] He is one of the more consequential figures of
the modern LLM era: a senior research leader at [[OpenAI]] through the rise of the
[[GPT-3|GPT]] models, and then the founder of the lab that became OpenAI's chief
safety-focused rival.

## Before Anthropic

Amodei worked at Baidu and then at Google before joining [[OpenAI]] in 2016, where
he became its **vice president of research** — a senior figure through the GPT-2
and GPT-3 era.[^wiki-amodei] Earlier that year he was the first author of
**"Concrete Problems in AI Safety,"** an influential paper that presents "a list of
five practical research problems related to accident risk" in machine-learning
systems — an early, concrete agenda for the safety questions that would come to
define his career.[^concrete2016]

## Anthropic

In 2021 Amodei and his sister [[Daniela Amodei]] were among a group who left OpenAI
"due to directional differences"; together with other former OpenAI staff they
founded [[Anthropic]].[^wiki-amodei] As CEO he leads the company behind the
[[Claude]] models and has become one of the most visible advocates of pairing rapid
AI progress with a safety-first posture.

That two-sided outlook runs through his 2024 essay *Machines of Loving Grace*, in
which he writes that "most people are underestimating just how radical the upside of
AI could be, just as I think most people are underestimating how bad the risks could
be."[^mlg2024]
