# DeepSeek

**DeepSeek** is a Chinese AI lab whose openly downloadable models reached near-frontier
quality and, in the process, forced a global rethink of how much AI has to cost and
where it can come from. Its significance is as much about **access** as raw capability:
a non-US lab shipping models anyone can download and build on. (For the model family
itself, see [[DeepSeek (models)]].)

## Origins

DeepSeek was founded in **July 2023** in Hangzhou by **Liang Wenfeng**, and is owned and
funded by his quantitative hedge fund, **High-Flyer**, having spun out of High-Flyer's
AGI research group.[^deepseek-wiki] Liang serves as CEO and holds a controlling majority
stake in the company.[^deepseek-wiki] For its first years the lab ran on High-Flyer's
capital rather than outside investment.

## The breakthrough moment

DeepSeek's defining episode came with the January 2025 release of its **R1** reasoning
model. On **January 27, 2025**, Nvidia's stock fell about 17%, shedding roughly $600
billion in market value — the largest single-day loss in U.S. market history — as
investors questioned whether cheap, openly available models undercut assumptions about
how much compute AI would require.[^cnbc-nvidia] The market drama is detailed in the
[[DeepSeek-R1 release]] event; what made it matter for the lab was the demonstration
that a non-US outfit could ship downloadable, near-frontier models. Like [[Mistral AI]],
it did so from outside the big US labs such as [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]].

## First outside funding

For most of its life DeepSeek took no external money. That changed in **2026**: by
**June 2026** it was reported to be finalizing its **first-ever external funding round** —
roughly **$7.4 billion** (over 50 billion yuan), at a post-money valuation reported at
**just under $60 billion** — with **Tencent** and battery maker **CATL** among the
backers and Liang himself committing some 20 billion yuan of his own
capital.[^deepseek-funding-scmp] It was a striking institutionalization for a lab that
had run on its founder's hedge-fund money.
