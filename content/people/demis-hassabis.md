# Demis Hassabis

**Demis Hassabis** (born 1976) is the co-founder and CEO of [[Google DeepMind]] and,
since 2024, a Nobel laureate in Chemistry. He is one of the central figures of modern
AI: a scientist-entrepreneur who built one of the field's leading labs and then won a
science Nobel for one of its results.

## From chess and neuroscience to DeepMind

Hassabis came to AI by an unusual route. A chess prodigy who reached master strength
as a teenager, he went on to earn a PhD in **cognitive neuroscience** from University
College London — a grounding in how the brain works that shaped DeepMind's
brain-inspired approach to building intelligence.[^hassabis-wiki]

In 2010 he co-founded **DeepMind** in London with Shane Legg and Mustafa Suleyman;
Google acquired the company in 2014 for a reported £400 million.[^hassabis-wiki]
Hassabis now leads the merged [[Google DeepMind]], which develops the [[Gemini]] and
[[Gemma]] model families — the through-line from his work to the modern LLM era.

## The 2024 Nobel Prize

In 2024 Hassabis shared the **Nobel Prize in Chemistry** with John Jumper for their
work on **protein structure prediction**, the achievement embodied in DeepMind's
[[AlphaFold]]; the prize's other half went to David Baker for computational protein
design.[^nobel-chem-2024][^hassabis-wiki] AlphaFold predicts the three-dimensional
shape of a protein from its amino-acid sequence accurately enough to be
transformative for biology — though the methods are a topic in their own
right.[^alphafold] The award is a neat parallel to [[Geoffrey Hinton]]'s 2024 Nobel
in Physics: two of that year's science Nobels recognizing artificial-intelligence
research. Hassabis was also knighted in 2024 for services to artificial
intelligence.[^hassabis-wiki]
