# Elon Musk

**Elon Musk** — the entrepreneur behind Tesla, SpaceX, and [[xAI]] — appears in the llmonomicon
for one reason: his part in the founding, and later unravelling, of [[OpenAI]]. (His other
ventures are out of scope here.)

## Co-founding OpenAI

Musk was a **co-founder and co-chair** of [[OpenAI]] at its launch in **2015**, and one of its
earliest funders, contributing roughly **$44 million** between 2016 and
2020.[^intro2015][^wiki-musk-altman] That backing was part of why OpenAI could begin as a
**well-resourced non-profit**, framed at the time as a counterweight to AI capability
concentrating inside a handful of companies.[^intro2015]

## The 2018 departure

The alliance didn't hold. In **early 2018**, "Musk proposed taking direct control of OpenAI,
either through a majority stake in a for-profit subsidiary or by folding the organization into
Tesla"; the other founders — among them [[Sam Altman]] — declined.[^wiki-musk-altman] Musk
**left the OpenAI board in February 2018**, "publicly citing a potential conflict of interest
with Tesla's growing artificial intelligence work."[^wiki-musk-altman]

## Musk v. Altman

Years later the split hardened into litigation. On **29 February 2024**, Musk sued OpenAI and
its executives, accusing them of "violating the company's founding agreement by prioritizing
profits over AI safety."[^wiki-musk-altman] The case reached a jury, and on **18 May 2026 the
jury found in favor of OpenAI** and its executives — but on narrow grounds: it held that
"Musk's claims were barred by the statute of limitations," meaning he had sued too late,
rather than deciding whether OpenAI had in fact breached the agreement.[^wiki-musk-altman] The
dispute is a window into how OpenAI's founding coalition — non-profit, open, and safety-first
by stated design — strained as the organization shifted toward a more commercial footing, the
very change at the heart of Musk's complaint.
