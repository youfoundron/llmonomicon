# Geoffrey Hinton

**Geoffrey Hinton** (b. 1947) is a British-Canadian computer scientist and
cognitive psychologist often called one of the "godfathers of deep learning."
His decades of work on neural networks laid groundwork that the
[[Transformer]] era would later build upon.

## Contributions

- Co-authored the influential 1986 work popularizing **backpropagation** for
  training multi-layer networks.
- Helped develop **Boltzmann machines** and, later, deep belief networks.
- Co-authored **AlexNet** (2012), whose decisive win on the ImageNet benchmark
  is widely credited with launching the modern deep learning boom.

## Recognition and later views

Hinton shared the 2018 **Turing Award** with Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun, and
in 2024 received the **Nobel Prize in Physics** (with John Hopfield) for
foundational discoveries in machine learning with neural networks. In 2023 he
left Google to speak more freely about the risks of advanced AI, becoming a
prominent voice on safety.
