# Google DeepMind

**Google DeepMind** is Google's frontier AI lab and the third of the field's
defining research organizations, alongside [[OpenAI]] and [[Anthropic]]. It was
formed in 2023 by merging two storied teams — DeepMind and Google Brain — and its
combined lineage runs straight through the history of modern language models: the
[[Transformer]] itself came out of Google Brain.

## The 2023 merger

On April 20, 2023, Google combined DeepMind and Google Research's Brain team into
"a single, focused unit called Google DeepMind," led by DeepMind co-founder
**[[Demis Hassabis]]** as CEO.[^gdm-announce] Announcing the merger, Hassabis
credited the "Brain and DeepMind teams" with having "laid much of the foundations
of the current AI industry, from Deep Reinforcement Learning to
Transformers."[^gdm-announce] He described the reorganization as the product of a
"fantastic partnership" with Sundar Pichai, [[Jeff Dean]], and James Manyika.[^gdm-announce]
The lab describes itself as bringing together "two of the world's leading AI labs — Google
Brain and DeepMind."[^gdm-about]

## Two lineages

**DeepMind** was founded in London in 2010 by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and
Mustafa Suleyman, and acquired by Google in 2014.[^gdm-wiki] Before the LLM era it
was best known for deep-reinforcement-learning landmarks such as AlphaGo and
AlphaFold.[^gdm-wiki]

**Google Brain** was Google's deep-learning research team, and its work is woven
through this wiki: the **word2vec** word-embedding method (2013),[^g-word2vec] the
**[[TensorFlow]]** machine-learning framework (open-sourced 2015),[^g-tensorflow] the
[[Transformer]] architecture (2017), and **[[BERT]]** (2018).[^g-bert]

## Today

Google DeepMind's stated mission is to "build AI responsibly to benefit
humanity."[^gdm-about] It develops the [[Gemini]] family of models, the
open-weights [[Gemma]] family, and the [[Imagen]] text-to-image line.[^gdm-wiki] Much of its
research is built on the [[JAX]] framework.[^deepmind-jax]
