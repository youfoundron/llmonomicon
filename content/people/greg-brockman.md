# Greg Brockman

**Greg Brockman** is a co-founder and the president of [[OpenAI]], and was its
first chief technology officer.[^gb-intro2015][^gb-wiki] Where [[Ilya Sutskever]]
was the scientific lead of OpenAI's founding group, Brockman has been its
engineering pillar — the person most responsible for building the systems and
large-scale compute its models run on.

## Before OpenAI

Brockman was the first chief technology officer of the payments company Stripe,
which he joined in 2013 and helped grow from a handful of employees to around two
hundred before leaving in 2015.[^gb-wiki] That experience scaling an engineering
organization is what he carried into OpenAI.

## At OpenAI

Brockman left Stripe to help **co-found OpenAI** in December 2015, alongside Sam
Altman, [[Ilya Sutskever]], and others.[^gb-intro2015][^gb-wiki] As CTO and later
president, he led much of the early engineering and large-scale compute work behind
the [[GPT-3|GPT]] models — the scale-up that ultimately made the [[ChatGPT launch]]
possible.[^gb-wiki]

In November 2023 he was swept up in the board's abrupt removal of CEO
[[Sam Altman]]: Brockman lost his board seat and resigned in protest, then returned
within days once Altman was reinstated.[^gb-news] The episode is covered in full as
the [[OpenAI board crisis]].
