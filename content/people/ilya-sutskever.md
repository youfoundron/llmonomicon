# Ilya Sutskever

**Ilya Sutskever** (born 1986) is an Israeli-Canadian computer scientist and a
co-founder and former chief scientist of [[OpenAI]].[^wiki-sutskever] He is one of
the clearest through-lines from the academic revival of deep learning to today's
large language models: a student in [[Geoffrey Hinton]]'s lab, a co-author of the
result that set off the deep-learning boom, and later the scientific lead behind
the models that became [[ChatGPT]].

## From AlexNet to sequence-to-sequence

Sutskever earned his PhD at the University of Toronto under [[Geoffrey
Hinton]].[^wiki-sutskever] With Hinton and Alex Krizhevsky he co-authored
**[[AlexNet wins ImageNet|AlexNet]]**, the convolutional network whose 2012 ImageNet
victory is widely credited with launching the modern deep-learning era.[^alexnet2012]
Two years later he was the lead author of **sequence-to-sequence learning**, an
encoder–decoder design that used one LSTM network to compress an input sequence
into a fixed-length vector and a second LSTM to unroll the output from it — a
recipe that directly anticipated modern generative sequence models.[^seq2seq2014]

## OpenAI

In December 2015 Sutskever left Google to help **co-found [[OpenAI]]**.[^intro2015]
As its chief scientist he led the research behind the [[GPT-3|GPT]] models and,
ultimately, the launch of [[ChatGPT]].[^wiki-sutskever]

In November 2023 he was part of the OpenAI board that briefly removed CEO
[[Sam Altman]]. He soon said he regretted his role, signed the employee letter
calling for Altman's return, and stepped down from the board once Altman was
reinstated.[^wiki-sutskever] The fuller story is its own entry: [[OpenAI board crisis]].

## Safe Superintelligence

Sutskever announced his departure from OpenAI in May 2024.[^wiki-sutskever] The
following month he co-founded **Safe Superintelligence Inc. (SSI)** with Daniel
Gross and Daniel Levy — a research lab he describes as having "one goal and one
product: a safe superintelligence."[^wiki-sutskever][^ssi]
