# Jack Clark

**Jack Clark** is a co-founder and the head of policy of [[Anthropic]] — and, through his
long-running newsletter **Import AI**, one of the most widely read chroniclers of the
language-model era. He sits where building frontier AI meets explaining it: a policy leader at one
of the major labs who also narrates the field's week-to-week research to a large public audience.

## From journalism to AI policy

Clark came to AI from **journalism** — by his own account "a technical journalist writing about
distributed systems, quantum computers, and AI research" before moving into the field he
covered.[^importai] He went on to become **Policy Director of [[OpenAI]]**, and in 2021 left to
co-found [[Anthropic]], where he is **head of policy**[^importai][^wiki-jackclark] — the
public- and government-facing side of an AI lab, a counterpart to the researchers and engineers
who build the models.

## Import AI and the AI Index

Clark's distinctive mark on how the field understands itself is **Import AI**, which he describes
as "a newsletter about AI research read by 70,000 people each week."[^importai] Its weekly digests
of papers and trends have made it a fixture for researchers and observers throughout the LLM era.
He also helped document the field more formally as a founding member of Stanford's **AI Index**
(2017–2024), the annual state-of-AI report.[^importai]
