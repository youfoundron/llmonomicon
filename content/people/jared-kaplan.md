# Jared Kaplan

**Jared Kaplan** is a co-founder and the Chief Science Officer of [[Anthropic]] — and, before
that, the lead author of the paper that gave the field its clearest argument for building
ever-larger models. He arrived at AI from theoretical physics, an unusual path that shaped his
most influential contribution.

## From physics to scaling laws

Kaplan is a physicist by training — an associate professor of physics and astronomy at Johns
Hopkins, working on quantum gravity and conformal field theory — who moved into machine learning,
joining [[OpenAI]] as a researcher in 2019.[^wiki-kaplan] There, in **2020**, he was the
**first author** of "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models," which found that "the loss scales
as a power-law with model size, dataset size, and the amount of compute used for training, with
some trends spanning more than seven orders of magnitude."[^kaplan2020] In plain terms: a
model's performance improves in a smooth, *predictable* way as you add parameters, data, and
compute — so scaling up is less a gamble than a forecastable bet. Those **[[Scaling laws|scaling
laws]]** became the intellectual backbone of the large-model era, including the [[GPT-3]] paper
Kaplan also co-authored.[^kaplan2020][^wiki-kaplan]

## Anthropic

In **2021**, Kaplan co-founded [[Anthropic]] — alongside [[Dario Amodei]] and others who had
left [[OpenAI]] — and serves as its **Chief Science Officer**, guiding the research behind the
[[Claude]] models.[^wiki-kaplan] In **October 2024** he was additionally named Anthropic's
**Responsible Scaling Officer**, overseeing the company's Responsible Scaling Policy and the
safety assessments required before a model is released.[^wiki-kaplan] There is a fitting
symmetry to it: the researcher who showed that capability scales predictably now helps decide
how cautiously to ride that curve.
