# Jeff Dean

**Jeff Dean** is Google's most storied engineer and the long-time leader of **Google Brain** —
the research lab where much of the company's deep-learning era began — and is now **Google's
Chief Scientist**. His place in the LLM story is institutional: he built and ran the lab, and
much of the infrastructure, that made large-scale AI at Google possible.

## Google Brain

Dean **co-founded Google Brain in 2011** and led it for more than a decade.[^wiki-jeffdean]
That lab was the institutional home of the [[Transformer]] — the architecture introduced in the
2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" by a team of Google researchers led by [[Ashish Vaswani]];
Dean ran the lab, but was **not** one of the paper's eight authors.[^vaswani2017] Google Brain
was also where **TensorFlow** was built — the open-source machine-learning framework Dean helped
design and Google released in 2015 — which became one of the standard tools for training large
models.[^wiki-jeffdean]

## Infrastructure and current role

Dean's bearing on LLMs is as much about **scale** as any single model. Long before the
deep-learning boom, he co-created some of the systems that define large-scale computing —
**MapReduce**, **Bigtable**, and **Spanner** — the distributed-systems groundwork that helps
make training enormous models feasible.[^wiki-jeffdean] After Google merged Brain and DeepMind
in **April 2023**, Dean became **Google's Chief Scientist**, "focusing on AI advances for
[[Google DeepMind]] and Google Research."[^google-jeffdean][^wiki-jeffdean]
