# John Schulman

**John Schulman** is a research scientist best known as the person behind the
reinforcement-learning techniques that made [[ChatGPT launch|ChatGPT]] work. A
co-founder of [[OpenAI]], he created the **PPO** algorithm and went on to lead the team
that turned [[RLHF|reinforcement learning from human feedback]] into a usable product.

## From PPO to ChatGPT

After a PhD in computer science at UC Berkeley (advised by Pieter Abbeel), Schulman
co-founded [[OpenAI]] in December 2015.[^schulman-wiki] In 2017 he led the work on
**Proximal Policy Optimization ([[PPO]])**, a reinforcement-learning algorithm prized
for being stable and simple to tune.[^ppo] PPO went on to become the workhorse of
**reinforcement learning from human feedback ([[RLHF]])** — the alignment technique
that shapes a raw language model into a helpful assistant.

At OpenAI, Schulman led the reinforcement-learning team behind ChatGPT and has been
described as its "architect."[^schulman-wiki] He was a co-author of the **InstructGPT**
work that productized RLHF — "training language models to follow instructions with
human feedback" — which directly preceded the [[ChatGPT launch]].[^instructgpt] The
mechanisms themselves belong to their own topics, [[PPO]] and [[RLHF]]; the
through-line from Schulman's research to ChatGPT is the point here.

## Later moves

In August 2024 Schulman left OpenAI to join [[Anthropic]]; in February 2025 he left
again to become chief scientist at Thinking Machines Lab.[^schulman-wiki]
