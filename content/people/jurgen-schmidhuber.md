# Jürgen Schmidhuber

**Jürgen Schmidhuber** (born 1963) is a German computer scientist whose work helped lay the
foundations of modern deep learning — and who is equally known for vocally disputing how
credit for that field has been assigned. He has been scientific director of the Swiss AI lab
**IDSIA** since 1995, and since 2021 has also directed the AI Initiative at **KAUST** in
Saudi Arabia.[^schmidhuber-wiki]

## Contributions

Schmidhuber's best-known result is the **[[LSTM]]** network, which he co-invented with his
student **[[Sepp Hochreiter]]** and published in 1997 — for two decades the dominant
architecture for sequence data.[^schmidhuber-wiki] His group also produced
**[[Fast weight programmers]]** (1991–92), a mechanism later recognized as an early relative
of the [[Attention|attention]] used in the [[Transformer]]; early work on "artificial
curiosity" and adversarial networks; and **Highway Networks** (2015), a precursor to the
residual connections that let very deep networks train.[^schmidhuber-wiki]

## The credit controversy

Schmidhuber is the field's most prominent voice on questions of priority and credit. He has
long **argued** that he and his collaborators were under-recognized relative to
**[[Geoffrey Hinton]]**, Yoshua Bengio, and Yann LeCun — the three who shared the 2018
Turing Award for deep learning. He published a pointed critique of their 2015 *Nature* review
of the field, contending that it failed to credit earlier pioneers, and maintains detailed
accounts of numerous priority disputes on his own pages — including a claim that his 1990s
work anticipated [[Generative adversarial networks|generative adversarial
networks]].[^schmidhuber-wiki][^schmidhuber-munich]

Others in the field reject the framing. Yann LeCun, for instance, told *The New York Times*
that "Jürgen is manically obsessed with recognition and keeps claiming credit he doesn't
deserve for many, many things"; Schmidhuber replied that LeCun did so "without any
justification, without providing a single example."[^schmidhuber-wiki] The dispute remains
contentious, with no clear consensus. This wiki takes no side — it notes only that the
arguments exist, and that Schmidhuber's own accounts are self-advocacy, to be read as
such.[^schmidhuber-wiki]
