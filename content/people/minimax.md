# MiniMax

**MiniMax** is a Shanghai-based AI company best known for its open-weight **M-series** language models and a run of popular consumer apps — the companion app Talkie, the video generator Hailuo AI, and others. Alongside [[Z.ai]], [[DeepSeek]], and [[Moonshot AI]], it is one of the leading Chinese labs treating [[Open weights|open-weight]] releases as a competitive strategy, and in January 2026 it became one of the first dedicated LLM developers to go public.

## Origins

MiniMax was founded in **December 2021** in Shanghai by **Yan Junjie**, a former SenseTime researcher and executive who holds a Ph.D. in AI from the Institute of Automation, Chinese Academy of Sciences and spent over six years at SenseTime before starting the company.[^wikipedia-minimax][^minimax-ir-management][^forbes-founder] He was soon joined by other SenseTime alumni, including **Zhou Yucong**, who came aboard in March 2022 and now leads MiniMax's visual-model research.[^minimax-ir-management] The company is registered as **Shanghai Xiyu Technology Co., Ltd.**, a name derived from the game-theory minimax algorithm.[^wikipedia-minimax]

## Consumer apps

MiniMax built its early audience through companion-chat apps before branching into generative media. **Glow**, an AI-character chat app, launched in October 2022 and reached 5 million users within four months, but was pulled from Chinese app stores in 2023.[^wikipedia-minimax] Its international counterpart, **Talkie**, launched in 2023 and became a genuine hit — Sensor Tower ranked it the fourth-most-downloaded app in the US in the first half of 2024, ahead of Character.AI — before briefly disappearing from the US Apple App Store in December 2024, a removal Apple attributed to unspecified "technical reasons."[^scmp-talkie-removed] A Chinese-market counterpart, **Xingye**, followed in September 2023.[^wikipedia-minimax]

The newer **Hailuo AI** product line launched in March 2024 as a text-and-music generation platform and added text-to-[[Video generation|video]] that September, becoming MiniMax's main generative-media brand alongside separate speech and music models, MiniMax Speech and MiniMax Music.[^wikipedia-minimax] In September 2025, Disney, Warner Bros. Discovery, and Universal Pictures sued MiniMax in US federal court (the Central District of California), alleging Hailuo AI let users generate copyrighted characters — Darth Vader, the Minions, Superman — at scale.[^hollywoodreporter-lawsuit] A federal judge denied MiniMax's motion to dismiss in May 2026, letting the case proceed toward discovery.[^disney-minimax-ruling]

## Models

MiniMax's language models trace back to the **abab** series that began in 2022, including the mixture-of-experts **ABAB 6.5** (April 2024). **MiniMax-01**, released in January 2025, was the company's first open-weight model, followed by the reasoning-focused **M1** (June 2025) and the agentic, coding-focused **M2** (October 2025). See [[MiniMax models]] for the full lineage.[^wikipedia-minimax][^turingpost-minimax]

## Funding and IPO

Early backers included **miHoYo**, the studio behind *Genshin Impact*.[^wikipedia-minimax] In March 2024, **Alibaba Group** led a funding round of more than **US$600 million**, valuing MiniMax above **$2.5 billion**, with **HongShan** (formerly Sequoia China), **Tencent**, **IDG Capital**, and **Hillhouse Investment** also participating.[^fortune-alibaba-funding] By 2025 its reported valuation approached **$4 billion**.[^turingpost-minimax]

MiniMax — one of China's so-called "AI Six Tigers"[^cnbc-minimax-zhipu] — listed on the **Hong Kong Stock Exchange** (ticker **0100**) on **January 9, 2026**, one day after [[Z.ai]]'s own listing. Cornerstone investors reportedly included the **Abu Dhabi Investment Authority** and **Eastspring Investments**, alongside continued backing from Alibaba.[^forbes-founder] The IPO priced at HK$165 a share and raised roughly **US$618 million**; shares closed their first day up about **109%**, valuing the company at around **HK$106.7 billion (US$13.7 billion)** and making Yan Junjie a billionaire.[^scmp-ipo-debut][^forbes-founder]

## After the IPO

The months after MiniMax's debut were eventful. On **February 23, 2026**, [[Anthropic]] said it had identified, "with high confidence," industrial-scale "distillation attack" campaigns against [[Claude]] by three Chinese AI labs — [[DeepSeek]], Moonshot AI, and MiniMax — allegedly using large networks of fraudulent accounts to extract training data in violation of its terms of service.[^anthropic-distill-primary] Anthropic said MiniMax's campaign was the largest of the three, flagging more than 13 million exchanges concentrated on agentic coding and tool use, with nearly half of that traffic reportedly redirecting to any newly released Anthropic model within 24 hours of release.[^anthropic-distill-primary][^anthropic-distill] This is an allegation by a competitor, not an adjudicated finding; see [[Knowledge distillation]] for the wider dispute.

MiniMax's stock kept climbing regardless, reaching a peak around **HK$1,330** in March 2026. By early **July 2026**, Forbes reported the stock had fallen more than **70%** from that peak, with Yan Junjie's paper net worth dropping from roughly **$12.6 billion back to $3.3 billion** — a decline attributed to investors rotating toward [[Z.ai]], whose stock had risen roughly 1,300% year to date, and to an approaching lock-up expiry.[^forbes-selloff-2026]
