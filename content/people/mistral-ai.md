# Mistral AI

**Mistral AI** is a Paris-based AI lab that, within months of its founding, became
the leading standard-bearer for **permissively-licensed open-weight** models — and
the most prominent European challenger to the US labs such as [[OpenAI]] and
[[Anthropic]].

## Founding

Mistral was founded on April 28, 2023, by three French researchers who had met at
the École Polytechnique: **[[Arthur Mensch]]** (CEO, formerly of [[Google
DeepMind]]), Guillaume Lample (chief scientist), and Timothée Lacroix (CTO) — the
latter two having worked on large models at [[Meta AI]].[^mistral-wiki] Its rise was
unusually fast: a roughly €105 million seed round in mid-2023 was followed by
successively larger raises, and by 2025 the company was reportedly valued in the
multiple billions of euros.[^mistral-wiki] (Funding figures move quickly; these are
as-reported, not current.)

## The open-weight strategy

Mistral's signature move was to release strong models under the **Apache 2.0**
license — genuinely unrestricted, unlike the custom community licenses of [[Llama
2]] or [[Gemma]]. As the company put it when releasing its first model, "We're
releasing Mistral 7B under the Apache 2.0 license, it can be used without
restrictions."[^mistral-7b] That model and its [[Mixture of Experts]] sibling Mixtral
are covered in [[Mistral 7B / Mixtral]]; both were distributed openly (Mixtral
famously via a bare BitTorrent link).[^mistral-wiki]

Alongside the open releases, Mistral runs a commercial business — a consumer
assistant (**Le Chat**), a developer API (**La Plateforme**), and proprietary
models — treating open weights as a strategy rather than giving everything
away.[^mistral-wiki]

## Why it matters

Mistral proved that a brand-new lab outside the United States could compete at the
frontier of open models. In doing so it made "European open AI" a genuine force, and
established the most credible permissive-license alternative to both the closed labs
and the use-restricted "open weight" releases of larger companies.[^mistral-wiki]
