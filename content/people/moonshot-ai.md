# Moonshot AI

**Moonshot AI** is a Beijing-based artificial-intelligence lab best known for its [[Kimi]] line of chatbots and open-weight language models. One of China's so-called "AI Six Tigers," it stands alongside [[DeepSeek]], [[Z.ai]], and [[MiniMax]] among the country's leading developers of [[Open weights|open-weight]] models, and by mid-2026 — in the midst of a rapid run-up toward a Hong Kong stock listing — it had shipped one of the largest open-weight models in the world.[^wikipedia-moonshot]

## Origins

Moonshot was founded in **March 2023** in Beijing by **Yang Zhilin** — its CEO — and Tsinghua University schoolmates **Zhou Xinyu** and **Wu Yuxin**; **Zhang Yutao** is also identified as a co-founder.[^wikipedia-moonshot][^technode-legaldispute-2024] Its English name comes from Pink Floyd's *The Dark Side of the Moon*, reportedly Yang's favorite album; its Chinese name, 月之暗面 ("dark side of the moon"), carries the same reference.[^wikipedia-moonshot][^techcrunch-alibaba-2024] Yang holds a bachelor's degree from Tsinghua and a Ph.D. from Carnegie Mellon, and is a co-author of the influential **XLNet** and **Transformer-XL** papers; he went on to lead the Wu Dao research effort at the Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence and to co-found an earlier startup, Recurrent AI.[^wikipedia-yangzhilin][^techcrunch-alibaba-2024]

That earlier venture became the source of a dispute: in **November 2024**, five of Recurrent AI's investors filed arbitration in Hong Kong against Yang and Zhang Yutao, alleging the pair launched Moonshot and raised funds in the same field without the investors' consent — a dispute that remained unresolved, per available reporting, as of mid-2026.[^technode-legaldispute-2024]

## Funding and IPO plans

Moonshot's valuation climbed quickly. A seed round of roughly $60 million valued it around $300 million; by **February 2024**, a Series B co-led by **Alibaba** and **HongShan** (formerly Sequoia China) raised more than **$1 billion** at a **$2.5 billion** valuation — at the time, the largest single funding round reported for a Chinese LLM developer.[^wikipedia-moonshot][^techcrunch-alibaba-2024] **Alibaba** and **Tencent** added about **$300 million** that August, at a post-money valuation above **$3.3 billion**, and **IDG Capital** led a **$500 million** Series C on **December 31, 2025**, oversubscribed by existing backers Alibaba and Tencent, that valued Moonshot at **$4.3 billion**.[^technode-tencent-2025oct][^technode-idg-2025]

The valuation then re-rated sharply through 2026 alongside Kimi's reception: reports put Moonshot near a $10 billion valuation early in the year, a targeted $18 billion by March, and a closed **$2 billion raise at a $20 billion valuation** — led by **Meituan's Long-Z Investments** — by **May 7**.[^pymnts-18b-2026][^techcrunch-meituan-2026] By **July 22, 2026**, TechNode reported a roughly **$31.5 billion** valuation and a final pre-IPO round said to be targeting up to **$50 billion**, ahead of a Hong Kong listing the company still expected within about six months; reported annual recurring revenue grew from roughly $100 million in March to about $300 million by June.[^technode-preipo-2026]

## Kimi and open weights

Moonshot's flagship product line, [[Kimi]], turned the company into a major force in [[Open weights|open-weight]] AI. **Kimi K2**, released openly in July 2025 with 1 trillion parameters, became a genuine hit — the most-downloaded model on Hugging Face the day after its release.[^wikipedia-moonshot] Its successor, the 2.8-trillion-parameter **Kimi K3**, followed on **16 July 2026**, billed as the largest open-weight model shipped to date; the line's technical detail is covered in the [[Kimi]] entry.[^tomshardware-k3][^investing-ipo-2026]

## Distillation allegation

On **February 23, 2026**, [[Anthropic]] said it had identified, "with high confidence," a distillation campaign against [[Claude]] that it attributed to Moonshot: more than 3.4 million exchanges routed through hundreds of fraudulent accounts, concentrated on agentic reasoning, coding, and computer use, with a later phase attempting to extract Claude's internal reasoning traces. Anthropic said it matched request metadata to the public profiles of senior Moonshot staff.[^anthropic-distill-primary] This is an allegation by a competitor, not an adjudicated finding; see [[Knowledge distillation]] for the wider dispute, which also named DeepSeek and MiniMax.
