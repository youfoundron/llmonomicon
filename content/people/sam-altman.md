# Sam Altman

**Sam Altman** (born 1985) is the co-founder and chief executive of [[OpenAI]],
and the organization's most prominent public voice across the period that brought
large language models into the mainstream.[^wiki] Before OpenAI he was president
of the startup accelerator Y Combinator (2014–2019), which he left to focus on
the company full-time.[^wiki]

## Co-founding OpenAI

OpenAI was founded in December 2015 as a nonprofit AI research lab, with Altman
among its co-founders — alongside figures such as [[Greg Brockman]] and
[[Ilya Sutskever]] — and one of its initial co-chairs.[^intro2015][^wiki] In 2019 the
organization restructured into a "capped-profit" entity to raise the capital
needed to train ever-larger models, and Altman became its CEO.[^openai-lp]

## Leading the company

As CEO, Altman has steered the developments that defined OpenAI's public era: its
partnership with Microsoft, the turn from a pure research lab toward products such
as the API and the [[ChatGPT launch|launch of ChatGPT]] in 2022, and the
company's high-profile framing of artificial general intelligence and its
risks.[^wiki] He became, in effect, the public face of the [[GPT-3|GPT]]-era LLM
boom.

## The November 2023 board crisis

In November 2023 OpenAI's board abruptly removed Altman as CEO, saying he "was
not consistently candid in his communications,"[^transition] but after an
employee revolt he returned within days, with the reversal finalized on November
29 alongside a new initial board.[^returns] The full episode has its own entry —
see [[OpenAI board crisis]].
