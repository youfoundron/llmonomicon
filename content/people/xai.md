xAI is the artificial-intelligence company [[Elon Musk]] founded to build [large language models](/concepts/) and chatbots, most notably the Grok product line. It has since become closely tied to Musk's other ventures: it absorbed the social network X (formerly Twitter) in 2025, was itself absorbed into SpaceX in 2026, and took on the corporate name SpaceXAI shortly after.

## Founding and mission

Musk incorporated xAI on March 9, 2023, assembling a founding team of AI researchers pulled from Google DeepMind, OpenAI, Google Research, and Tesla, including chief engineer Igor Babuschkin, formerly of DeepMind[^wiki-xai]. He announced the company publicly on July 12, 2023[^cnbc-launch]. xAI is headquartered in the San Francisco Bay Area, at Stanford Research Park in Palo Alto[^wiki-xai].

Musk described xAI's goal as building AI that seeks to "understand the true nature of the universe," framing it as a "maximally truth-seeking" alternative to rivals such as [[OpenAI]], which he characterized as overly cautious and politically correct[^euronews-mission].

## Infrastructure

To train its models, xAI built a supercomputer cluster nicknamed "Colossus" in Memphis, Tennessee. It launched in October 2024 with 100,000 Nvidia H100 GPUs, assembled in just 122 days, with plans announced at the time to double its capacity to 200,000 GPUs[^nvidia-colossus].

## Acquisition of X, and merger with SpaceX

In March 2025, xAI acquired the social network X (formerly Twitter) in an all-stock deal that valued X at $33 billion and xAI itself at $80 billion, putting the combined company, xAI Holdings Corp., at $113 billion[^tc-x-acquisition].

Less than a year later, on February 2, 2026, Musk's rocket company SpaceX merged with xAI in another all-stock transaction. The deal valued SpaceX at roughly $1 trillion and xAI at $250 billion, for a combined valuation of about $1.25 trillion, ahead of SpaceX's own planned initial public offering[^fool-merger].

## Rebrand to SpaceXAI

Following SpaceX's initial public offering, xAI's corporate entity completed a rebrand to "SpaceXAI" around July 6–7, 2026. The change affected the corporate name rather than its consumer products: the Grok chatbot brand itself was unaffected and kept its name[^engadget-rebrand].
