# Midjourney

**Midjourney** is a proprietary **text-to-image** service known for a distinctive,
highly-polished house aesthetic. Together with [[DALL·E]] and the open-weights
[[Stable Diffusion]], it defined the consumer text-to-image era — and it occupies a
particular niche within it: the **closed, subscription, aesthetics-first** option, run by a
small independent lab rather than a big AI company or an open community.

## Origins

Midjourney, Inc. was founded in San Francisco by **David Holz**, previously a co-founder of
the gesture-control company Leap Motion.[^mj-wiki] It entered **open beta on July 12, 2022**,
and Holz said the company was **profitable within weeks**.[^mj-wiki] Unusually for such a
prominent AI company, Midjourney grew as a small, independent operation rather than a heavily
venture-funded startup. For its first two years it ran entirely through a **Discord bot** —
you typed prompts as chat commands — and only added a standalone **web interface in August
2024**.[^mj-wiki]

## Models and cadence

Midjourney ships new image models frequently. The line ran from V5 (2023) through V6 and the
web-enabled **V6.1** (July 2024) to **V7** (April 2025) and the V8 generation: **V8** arrived
in March 2026, and **V8.1** followed in April 2026, becoming the default in **June 2026**
with HD 2K output and roughly 4–5× faster rendering.[^mj-version] (Because the cadence is so
quick, any "current version" dates quickly.) In **June 2025** Midjourney also released its
first **video** model, V1, which animates a still image into a short clip — its entry into
[[Video generation]].[^mj-video]

## Why it matters

Midjourney is the closed, curated counterpoint in the image-generation landscape: where
[[Stable Diffusion]] is open-weights and community-driven and [[DALL·E]] was API- and
ChatGPT-first, Midjourney is a subscription product with a strong, opinionated visual style,
built by a small independent team. Like its peers it is [[Diffusion models|diffusion]]-based,
but its exact architecture is unpublished, so it is best understood by what it does rather
than how it works.
